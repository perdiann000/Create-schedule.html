The Golden Age of Cartoons?

Most of us definitely watched cartoons as a kid, but there's still a vast majority of older people who still watch them. Just because they're animated doesn't mean older people can't enjoy them. But I'd say the 90's was a real golden age of cartoons. Sorry Disney, I love your movies and shows but to me they lack something what older cartoons (Mostly Cartoon Network) shows had.

(A few) Iconic 90's Cartoons

Spongebob Squarepants

Who lives in a pineapple under the sea? It's everyone's favorite sponge! Don't deny it, he was a big part of your childhood and to this very day, he's still trending. Especially in memes. But back to the main point. He's one of the most recognizable characters in the animated industry and always sports a smile on my face!

Johnny Bravo

A muscular, narcissistic, and dim-witted womanizer tries to get with the ladies but always fails miserably. He has this Elvis like voice that's actually amusing to listen to with the comedic sense from the show. Even though I feel bad whenever he gets rejected, he still manages to make the moments laughable.

Ed, Edd n Eddy

Three preteen boys Ed, Edd and Eddy, live in a surburban cul-de-sac. Eddy, who claims to be the ruler of the trio drags his friends into inventing schemes to make money so they could buy their favorite candy of all, jawbreakers. It has a style of a slapstick cartoon and lotsa exaggerated expressions, my type of show!

The Grim Adventures of Billy and Mandy

Billy and Mandy are a mismatched pair of friends who befriend the Grim Reaper after winning a limbo game against him. The three of them go on misadventures through school to spirit worlds.
